Peanut Butter Cake

This recipe is a testament to the irresistible allure of peanut butter in dessert form. With its moist, tender crumb and rich peanut butter flavor, this cake is a true crowd-pleaser. Whether you're celebrating a special occasion or simply craving a comforting slice of homemade goodness, this cake is sure to satisfy. Best of all, it's easy to make and requires just a handful of pantry staples. So, roll up your sleeves, preheat your oven, and get ready to embark on a baking adventure that'll leave your taste buds singing with joy.

How to make Peanut Butter Cake

To craft this scrumptious Peanut Butter Cake, begin by preheating your oven to 325 degrees F (165 degrees C) and greasing and flouring two 9inch round baking pans. In a large mixing bowl, cream together 1 cup of creamy peanut butter and a half cup of butter until smooth. Gradually add in 4 cups of confectioners' sugar, beating well after each addition, until the mixture is light and fluffy. Beat in 4 eggs one at a time, ensuring each egg is fully incorporated before adding the next. Now, alternate adding 2 cups of all-purpose flour and 2 teaspoons of baking powder with 1 cup of milk, beginning and ending with the flour mixture, and mixing until just combined. Pour the batter into the prepared baking pan, spreading it out evenly. Bake in the preheated oven for 35 to 40 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Once baked, allow the cake to cool completely in the pan on a wire rack. Serve slices of this delectable Peanut Butter Cake as is, or add a dollop of whipped cream or a drizzle of chocolate sauce for an extra touch of indulgence. Enjoy every moist, peanut buttery bite!

Ingredients

Cake

  • ½ cup creamy peanut butter
  • ½ cup butter, softened
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 (15.25 ounce) package butter cake mix
  • ⅔ cup water

Frosting

  • 1 cup creamy peanut butter
  • ½ cup butter, softened
  • 4 cups confectioners' sugar
  • ⅓ cup heavy cream

Directions

  1. Preheat the oven to 325 degrees F (165 degrees C). Grease and flour two (9-inch) round cake pans.

  2. Make cake: Beat peanut butter and butter in a mixing bowl until light and fluffy. Add eggs, one at time, mixing well after each addition. Add cake mix alternately with water, stirring until just combined. Pour batter into the prepared pans.

  3. Bake in the preheated oven until a toothpick inserted into the centers comes out clean, about 25 minutes. Allow cakes to cool in the pans for 10 minutes, then turn out onto a wire rack to cool completely before frosting.

  4. Make frosting: Beat peanut butter and butter in a mixing bowl until light and fluffy. Add confectioner's sugar; mix until combined. Beat in enough cream until frosting reaches spreading consistency.

  5. Fill and frost cooled cake layers with frosting.
