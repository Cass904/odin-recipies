Peanut Butter Bars

If you are wanting an easy recipe, look no further. These no-bake peanut butter bars are perfect for satisfying your sweet cravings without breaking a sweat in the kitchen. With just a handful of ingredients and no oven required, these bars come together effortlessly in just 20 minutes of prep time. Whether you need a quick dessert for a potluck, a snack to fuel your afternoon, or a treat to indulge in after dinner, these peanut butter bars are sure to become a staple in your recipe collection. So, grab your mixing bowl and let's whip up a batch of these irresistible delights!

How to make Peanut Butter Bars

To make these irresistible peanut butter bars, start by melting 1 cup of butter or margarine in a medium saucepan over medium heat. Once melted, stir in 2 cups of graham cracker crumbs until well combined. Next, remove the saucepan from heat and mix in 2 cups of confectioners' sugar and 1 cup of creamy peanut butter until smooth. Press this peanut butter mixture firmly into the bottom of an ungreased 9x13-inch pan, creating an even layer. Then, in a small saucepan over low heat or in the microwave, melt 1 ½ cups of semisweet chocolate chips with ¼ cup of creamy peanut butter, stirring frequently until smooth. Pour the melted chocolate mixture evenly over the peanut butter layer in the pan, using a spatula to spread it out to the edges. Place the pan in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours until the bars are firm and the chocolate topping is set. Once chilled, slice the bars into squares or rectangles of your desired size using a sharp knife, and they're ready to serve. These peanut butter bars can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to one week, although they're often eaten long before then! These delightful no-bake peanut butter bars are sure to be a hit with everyone who tries them!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 2 cups confectioners' sugar
  • 1 cup butter or margarine, melted
  • 1 cup peanut butter
  • 1 ½ cups semisweet chocolate chips
  • 4 tablespoons peanut butter

Directions

  1. Prepare the Base: In a medium saucepan over medium heat, melt 1 cup of butter or margarine and 2 cups of graham cracker crumbs. Stir well until combined.

  2. Add Peanut Butter and Sugar: Remove the saucepan from heat and stir in 2 cups of confectioners' sugar and 1 cup of creamy peanut butter until the mixture is smooth and well blended.

  3. Press into Pan: Press the peanut butter mixture firmly into the bottom of an ungreased 9x13-inch pan, creating an even layer. A spatula or the back of a spoon can help smooth the surface.

  4. Prepare the Topping: In a small saucepan over low heat or in the microwave, melt 1 ½ cups of semisweet chocolate chips with ¼ cup of creamy peanut butter. Stir frequently until smooth and well combined.

  5. Spread the Topping: Once the chocolate mixture is melted and smooth, pour it evenly over the peanut butter layer in the pan, using a spatula to spread it out to the edges.

  6. Chill: Place the pan in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours, or until the bars are firm and the chocolate topping is set.

  7. Slice and Serve:Once chilled, use a sharp knife to cut the bars into squares or rectangles of your desired size. Serve and enjoy!
